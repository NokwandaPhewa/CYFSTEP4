Ujeqe (Steamed Bread)

  • Time: 2 h
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients Quantity
Maize Meal 2 cups
All-purpose Flour 2 cups
Baking Powder 2 tsp
Salt 1 tsp
Sugar 1 tbsp
Warm Water 1 ½ cups
Yeast 1 packet

Step 1: In a bowl, mix the maize meal, all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt.

Step 2: In another bowl, dissolve the sugar and yeast in warm water. Add to the dry mixture and knead until smooth.

Step 3: Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let it rise for 1 hour. Once risen, place the dough in a pot and steam for about 1 hour.

Amathumbu (Tripe)

  • Time: 1 h 30 m
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Beef Tripe (cleaned) 1 kg
Onion 1 large, chopped
Tomatoes 2, chopped
Garlic 3 cloves, minced
Chili Peppers 2, chopped (optional)
Ginger 1 tsp, grated
Vegetable Oil 2 tbsp
Salt to taste

Step 1: Rinse the tripe thoroughly and cut it into small pieces.

Step 2: In a large pot, heat the oil and sauté the onions, garlic, ginger, and chili peppers until soft.

Step 3: Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they release their juice.

Step 4: Add the tripe, cover with water, and let it cook for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until tender. Add salt to taste.

Bunny Chow

  • Time: 1 h
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Curry (Chicken, Lamb, or Veg) 2 cups
Bread Loaf 1 large, unsliced
Oil 2 tbsp
Garlic 2 cloves, minced
Onion 1 large, chopped
Tomatoes 2, chopped
Chili Peppers 1, optional

Step 1: Slice off the top of the bread loaf and hollow it out, leaving enough bread to hold the curry.

Step 2: Prepare the curry by sautéing onions, garlic, and chili peppers in oil until soft. Add tomatoes, then your choice of meat or vegetables and cook until done.

Step 3: Spoon the curry into the hollowed bread and serve hot.
